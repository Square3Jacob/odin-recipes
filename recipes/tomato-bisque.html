Tomato Bisque Recipe!

This tomato bisque is proof you don't need a whole basket of vine-ripened tomatoes to make delicious tomato bisque. This recipe uses canned tomatoes. Rice makes this soup silky, not starchy.

What you will need:

  • 1 tablespoon Olive Oil
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 3 cloved garlic, chopped
  • 1 (32 ounce) container chicken broth
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed San Marizano tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 pinch freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 3 tablespoons uncooked jasmine rice
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons basil leaves

Directions:

Step 1: Gather your ingredients.

Step 2: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat; cook and stir onion and celery with a pinch of salt until onion is translucent, 5 to 8 minutes. Stir in garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Step 3: Pour chicken broth and tomatoes into onion mixture. Bring to a simmer and season with paprika, black pepper, and cayenne pepper.

Step 4: Turn heat to low and stir rice into soup; skim foam occasionally and simmer until rice and vegetables are very tender, 35 to 45 minutes. Add sugar.

Step 5: Blend soup with an immersion blender in the pot until smooth.

Step 6: Whisk 1/2 cup cream into soup and season with salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, and sugar if needed. If soup is too thick, add more broth; if soup is too thin, cook, stirring often, until reduced and slightly thickened, about 10 minutes.

Step 7: Ladle into warmed bowls; garnish each bowl with a drizzle of cream and top each with about 1 teaspoon chopped basil.
